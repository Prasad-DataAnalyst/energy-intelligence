Why asset age matters
Gulf offshore infrastructure was largely built in the 1980s and 1990s. Assets exceeding 25 years require more frequent inspection cycles, specialised corrosion assessment and increasingly AI-assisted anomaly detection to maintain safe operation.
Inspection frequency gap
Industry standard recommends major platform inspection every 5 years. Estimated backlog suggests a significant proportion of Gulf assets are overdue — conflict disruption has compounded pre-existing delays.
UAV inspection advantage
Traditional rope-access inspection of a large offshore platform takes 4-6 weeks. UAV-based inspection with optical gas imaging completes equivalent coverage in 3-5 days with real-time data capture and immediate anomaly flagging.

UAV-first inspection protocol — the correct operational approach
For damaged offshore infrastructure, deploying personnel for initial assessment carries significant safety risk. UAV-first inspection — using optical gas imaging, thermal cameras and LiDAR — provides safe standoff damage assessment before any human access is authorised. This is not just more efficient — it is the operationally correct safety sequence. The AIMS framework methodology directly supports this approach.
