Key constraint
CEO stated restart takes weeks minimum. Strait of Hormuz must be navigable before cargoes can move. Reports indicate significant capacity may be sidelined — Multi-year repair estimate per CEO statement to Reuters — subject to damage assessment.
Ceasefire — market reaction
TTF gas prices reportedly fell ~20% following ceasefire announcement. Oil declined. Insurance costs dropping. Market interpretation: pricing may reflect optimism rather than confirmed supply restoration — actual rebalancing typically takes months.
Strait of Hormuz reopening
Shipping must resume before any LNG cargoes leave Ras Laffan. Saudi East-West pipeline (7mb/d) is reported as the primary bypass route — no LNG bypass exists for Qatar based on available information. Hormuz dependency is a key scenario variable.
Repair supply chain challenge
Restoring Ras Laffan requires specialised LNG train components with 12–18 month procurement lead times. Historical precedent suggests supply chain recovery for LNG components takes months — this is a scenario assumption, not confirmed timeline.

US LNG has been widely reported as a beneficiary of the supply disruption. Market share dynamics are a scenario interpretation — diversification trends may persist post-crisis based on buyer behaviour patterns.
Energy transition accelerating
Philippines, India and China all accelerated solar and battery deployments during the crisis. The disruption appears to be accelerating both energy security and decarbonisation investment — this is an analyst interpretation of reported market signals.
OGMP 2.0 — restart condition
Methane transparency expectations are likely to rise among European buyers — OGMP 2.0 Level 4-5 compliance may become a reinstatement condition based on existing EU regulatory direction. This represents a potential demand signal for integrated inspection and emissions audit services.
